GRiP

How To Sell More Through The Rules Of Interpersonal Attraction

GRiP is a simple tool built to allow brands to better understand modern consumers. By assessing their performance in the dimensions of Generosity, Responsiveness, and Proximity, brands can more effectively market and sell to their customers.

Interpersonal Attraction

GRiP is built on interpersonal attraction: a category of study in the field of Social Psychology that deals with the attraction of one person to another.

In the theory of Interpersonal Attraction, five key dimensions generally stand out as affecting the likelihood of one individual liking and being attracted to another.

Next: Applying to Brand

It's clear that brands have human–like qualities and that we sometimes consider brands in much the same manner as we consider other people.

If we accept that we are drawn almost preternaturally to certain brands, then it seems logical to assess our relationship with brands in much the same way as we would evaluate relationships between one person and another.

Next: Dimensions of GRiP

We can distill the five dimensions of interpersonal attraction into three easily understood and easily measured variables that we believe help explain the buying dynamic between consumers and brands.

  • G

    Generosity

  • R

    Responsiveness

  • P

    Proximity

Together, these variables form the acronym 'GRiP'. We believe that when we measure these three variables via their underlying drivers, we will get a specific read on what areas a brand needs to focus on to increase their buyability for consumers.

Next: Generosity
G

Generosity

How much do you care? How much do you give to me and the society I live in?

Consumers are drawn to a brand that is generous both to individuals and to society. Generous brands not only make it their mission to improve the lives of their customers but they also ave a social mission, not just a position in the category.

Thus a brand's generosity of spirit is a reflection of their inner world: a reflection of their values, culture, point of view on the human experience, and their drive to make society better in some way.

Next: Responsiveness
R

Responsiveness

How well do you know me?

Responsiveness is all about the brand's intimate understanding of customers. It differs from generosity in a very crucial way. Whereas generosity can benefit consumers en masse, or society in toto, responsiveness is more of an individual or granulated understanding or meeting of specific needs, and less of a generalised out-pouring of good will or lagresse.

Next: Proximity
P

Proximity

How close are you to me?

A brand's proximity to a consumer is what gives the brand the greatest ability to engage, educate, and sell. Proximity for our GRiP model is less about "getting the message out" and more about multi-dimensional touch points – the creative use of media to create brand experiences in the lives of consumers.

GRiP evaluates a brand's proximity by looking at its presence across four key physical dimensions: Me, My World, My Community, and The World. These dimensions go from an already engaged customer (Me), out to passive, traditional advertising (The World).
